Discover Indian Art Forms

This page is part of the game created by Team Vriddhi, NPS Jayanagar, Bengaluru — a guided tour of India's performing, visual, and craft traditions that appear in the game.

Overview

India's art forms are diverse, regionally rooted, and full of stories, techniques, and values. In the game, players encounter challenges inspired by dance, music, crafts, theatre, and folk traditions. Each encounter aims to teach cultural context while encouraging strategic thinking and teamwork.

Types of art forms (please click the artform name to explore more)

  1. Mandala Art — Mandala art is a symbolic circular design that originated in Hindu and Buddhist traditions to represent the universe's wholeness and interconnectedness. The term "mandala" means "circle" in Sanskrit. These artworks typically feature intricate geometric patterns that radiate from a central point and were traditionally used for meditation and sacred rituals. Creating mandalas is now a popular practice for its meditative and therapeutic benefits, promoting focus, mindfulness, and relaxation.

Major art forms featured

Classical & Folk Dance

From Bharatanatyam's rhythmic storytelling to vibrant folk dances, dance sequences in the game teach rhythm, symbolism, and cultural context. Tasks may ask players to identify gestures, match rhythms, or recreate simple sequences as team puzzles.

Music & Instruments

Explore classical ragas, folk songs, and traditional instruments. Gameplay uses short audio cues and recognition challenges to promote listening skills and cultural appreciation.

Handicrafts & Visual Arts

Textiles, pottery, rangoli, and painting traditions appear as visual puzzles and creation challenges that reward observation, pattern recognition, and creativity.

Theatre & Storytelling

Folk tales and classic dramas are used to present moral dilemmas and strategy scenarios. Players decode clues from stories to make team decisions and progress.

Traditional Games & Play

Simple traditional games inspire in-game mini-games that teach rules, turn-taking, and collaborative tactics — aligning with the project's larger learning objectives.

Interactive learning & activities

  • Listen & match: Short audio clips of instruments or songs — match to region or emotion.
  • Pattern puzzles: Recreate a rangoli or textile motif as a timed team exercise.
  • Story clues: Decode a folktale to reveal a strategy that helps your team advance.
