Overview

India's art forms are diverse, regionally rooted, and full of stories, techniques, and values. In the game, players encounter challenges inspired by dance, music, crafts, theatre, and folk traditions. Each encounter aims to teach cultural context while encouraging strategic thinking and teamwork.

Types of art forms (click to explore)

  1. Mandala Art — Mandala art is a symbolic circular design that originated in Hindu and Buddhist traditions to represent the universe's wholeness and interconnectedness. The term “mandala” means “circle” in Sanskrit. These artworks typically feature intricate geometric patterns radiating from a central point and were traditionally used for meditation and sacred rituals. Creating mandalas is popular today for its meditative and therapeutic benefits, promoting focus, mindfulness, and relaxation.
  2. Madhubani (Mithila) Painting — A folk art tradition from Bihar’s Mithila region, characterized by intricate line work and vibrant fills. Traditionally painted on mud walls and floors, it is now created on paper, cloth, and canvas using fingers, twigs, brushes, and nib pens. Themes span mythology, nature, and daily life, featuring motifs like deities, animals, flowers, and geometric patterns, often colored with natural dyes derived from turmeric, henna, and charcoal.

Madhubani (also known as Mithila painting) is a traditional folk art from the Mithila region of Bihar, characterized by intricate patterns and vibrant colors. Originally painted on mud walls and floors, it is now practiced on paper, cloth, and canvas using fingers, twigs, brushes, and nib pens. Subjects include mythological and religious scenes as well as nature, with common motifs such as gods, animals, flowers, and geometric patterns. Colors are often derived from natural sources like turmeric, henna, and charcoal.

Major art forms featured

Classical & Folk Dance

From Bharatanatyam's rhythmic storytelling to vibrant folk dances, dance sequences in the game teach rhythm, symbolism, and cultural context. Tasks may ask players to identify gestures, match rhythms, or recreate simple sequences as team puzzles.

Music & Instruments

Explore classical ragas, folk songs, and traditional instruments. Gameplay uses short audio cues and recognition challenges to promote listening skills and cultural appreciation.

Handicrafts & Visual Arts

Textiles, pottery, rangoli, and painting traditions appear as visual puzzles and creation challenges that reward observation, pattern recognition, and creativity.

Theatre & Storytelling

Folk tales and classic dramas present moral dilemmas and strategy scenarios. Players decode clues from stories to make team decisions and progress.

Traditional Games & Play

Simple traditional games inspire in-game mini-games that teach rules, turn-taking, and collaborative tactics — aligning with the project's larger learning objectives.
